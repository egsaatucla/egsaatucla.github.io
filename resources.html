eGSA Lounge

  • Located in Boelter Hall 2438 (South East corner of Boelter Hall)
  • The lounge is open to all members 24/7 unless reserved. If you'd like to reserve the lounge for a special occasion or event, download request form to book the eGSA Lounge
  • The lounge is equipped with a security lock and can only be opened by entering your approved 10 digit department ID in the keypad. In addition, there are security cameras in the lounge to monitor and record all activities. If you do not yet have the access code to the lounge, you may request it with an email to the lounge manager.
  • Some features: Separate study room, and recreation area (Wii, PS2, air hockey, foosball table)
  • Lounge rules:
    • Be responsible
    • Lounge door MUST be closed at all times
    • Non EGSA patrons are not allowed in the lounge
    • No sleeping in the lounge
    • No lewd conduct
    • No smoking
    • Dispose of your own trash
    • We reserve the right to refuse anyone the use of EGSA lounge and equipment
    • We reserve the right to add, change, or update the rules at any time without prior notification
    • Failure to comply with these rules will result in loss of priviledge of using the lounge for the entire academic year

Maps and Transportation

  • Official campus map: Interactive (and better than the old map), but still in beta
  • UCLA Transportation & Parking: The office for campus Parking, Permits, Commuting, RideShare, VanPool, FlexCar, RideMatch, Carpools, BruinGo
  • Google Map: The interface you know and love, now with campus buildings labeled
  • Bruin Bus: Free shuttle service available for the campus area
  • FlyAway: Shuttle service to and from LAX airport
  • Los Angeles County MTA: Bus and light rail to get you around LA County
  • Culver City Bus and Santa Monica Bus: The Big Green Bus and Big Blue Bus, respectively, which are particularly useful around here

Studying and Research

  • Grad Division’s list of resources: A comprehensive list of official resources
  • Graduate Student Resource Center: A whole office dedicated to helping you with grad school
  • Graduate Writing Center: Under the purview of the GSRC, the GWC is here to help you with your paper or thesis
  • GRAPES: Graduate and Postdoctoral Extramural Support Database
  • Office of Intellectual Property: This is where you go when you have a brilliant idea that will make you a gazillion dollars
  • UCLA Science and Engineering Library: (Most) Everything you need for research

Sports and Health

  • UCLA Intramural Sports: eGSA encourages engineering graduate students to participate in intramural sports as much as possible. Every quarter eGSA sponsors a handful of teams over a variety of sports. Visit the UCLA IMs website to see upcoming sports. Email the IM manager with any other questions.
  • UCLA Recreation: The group that runs the Wooden Center, the Marina Aquatic Center, etc. – check out the Rec Quarterly for a full list of activities
  • Student Health Services: Information on your health insurance plan and the services available at Ashe Center
  • The Counseling Center: These folks are here to help you with the stress of grad school

Events

  • eGSA Event Calendar: Various events, including eGSA Town Hall and Socials. These events are a great way to get out of your lab, meet people who aren't engineers, and enjoy some free food and alcohol
  • UCLA Central Ticket Office: Easy way to access campus and local events

Other
